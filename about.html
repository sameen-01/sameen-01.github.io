About me

A bit more about who I am, what I like working on, and what I do outside of code.

Who I am

I’m a third-year Computer Science student at Wilfrid Laurier University, interested in systems, computer networks, and RISC-V / low-level architecture.

I like understanding how things really work under the hood — how bytes move across a network, how instructions are executed, and how small implementation details can affect performance and behaviour.

What I’m into in CS

I tend to enjoy projects that involve:

  • Working close to the hardware or OS (RISC-V, emulators, digital logic).
  • Networking and socket-level programming (TCP/UDP, packet behaviour, Linux tools).
  • Debugging and tracing issues rather than just using libraries as a black box.
Systems programming Networks RISC-V Digital logic

Outside of CS

Outside of coursework and projects, I’m usually doing some mix of hanging out with friends, listening to music, gaming, or going down random YouTube rabbit holes about hardware, networking, and tech.

I like environments where people are direct, curious, and okay with saying “I don’t know yet, but I’ll figure it out.”
